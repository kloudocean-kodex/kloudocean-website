About

Senior migration expertise, without the Fortune 500 price tag or the procurement maze.

Why KloudOcean exists

Most Teradata migrations don't fail because of data volume. They fail because of everything volume hides — undocumented BTEQ logic, silent charset issues, dependency chains nobody wrote down, and validation gaps that surface three weeks after go-live.

That kind of judgment — the kind that only comes from actually running these migrations, not just architecting them on a whiteboard — is usually locked inside large consulting firms, priced for enterprise budgets, and delivered by whoever's available rather than whoever's senior.

KloudOcean exists to make that same level of rigor available to growing businesses directly, at a fixed, honest price, without a procurement process standing in the way.

What we've actually run

KloudOcean's delivery patterns come from enterprise-scale programs across retail, insurance, and banking — 1,000+ TB migrated, 5,000+ tables moved, over a decade of combined GCP data engineering experience on the bench. We don't name specific past client engagements that aren't ours to name, but the scale and the scar tissue are real, and it's what every assessment and every migration plan we write is built on.

KODE-X — our training bench

Alongside client delivery, KloudOcean runs KODE-X, a data engineering training program for engineers entering the field. It means KloudOcean's delivery bench isn't limited to one calendar — trained associates work under senior review on real engagements, and the best of each cohort becomes part of how we scale delivery without diluting quality.

How we work

Every engagement starts under NDA. Scope and price are agreed before any work begins. And the assessment is run by the same senior team that delivers the migration — not a name that disappears after the sales call.

Where we work

Based in Pune, India, with clients across India, Australia, the UAE, the USA, the UK, and Canada. All coordination happens over video calls and shared documentation, with overlap hours arranged around your time zone.
